Min Yoongi to Suga

He got accepted as a trainee at a below average(almost on the edge of bankruptcy) entertainment agency called Big Hit entertainment. Originally, he joined the agency to become a music producer, but later on, fate lead him to become an Idol(Singer + performer). He became a member of a under training boy-band called Bangtan boys(BTS), which includes a total of seven members. He debuted as one of leading rappers with the stage name "Suga" on 13th june 2013, with the track called "No More Dream". In earlier years of his debut, he went through depression due to pressure of K-Pop Music industry and lack of success on Music charts.

Suga switching to the reversed version of Suga

He sought cure for his depression everywhere, but at last, he found it in his own words and music. He then released his first Solo Mix tape called "Agust D" on 15th of August 2016. Agust D, first four letters of it are the opposite version of his stage name Suga. And T D stand for his underground rap crew Town Daegu, he was a part of it before being an idol. In his mix tape, he was blunt with his words, letting out his emotions and the causes of his depression. His mix tape was a huge success and this gave him another stage name Agust D along with Suga. Suga talks about softness, love and life, whereas Agust D spits the facts and the ugly side of society. Agust D is not the negative or vicious side of him, its just he hints and highlights the problems(everyone faces) with his music and songs, using this side. Agust D points out the problem and Suga(along with BTS) finds the solution for it. Suga worked as a rapper, writer and producer in all of the albums of BTS. He has also collaborated with several popular Artists from K-Pop and American music industries. On 22nd may 2020, he released his second solo mix tape "D-2" as a comeback of Agust D, showing his viewpoint on current circumstances of society and world.
